Country Kitchen Anchorage, AK. 01-02-2011.

Welcome to our Monday review this not only is our first review of 2012, it is also the first review to be posted on our brand new website! This website will allow you to follow Alaska Burger Blog in a more structured way, with the new website being home for all of our reviews and pictures. We will continue to have our Facebook page so fans can interact with each other and us. Now we have a website, if you already haven’t be sure to join us on Facebook or follow us on Twitter! Also go to the “Burger Map” link and see a map of all the places we visited!

Now for today, we followed the tip of a local who overheard us while eating at a burger place last week. They said a place in downtown Anchorage with good burgers is the Country Kitchen. So we followed this tip and stopped at the Country Kitchen located on 5th Avenue.

Upon entering the place is that it has a vibe of a breakfast eatery like Denny’s or IHOP. We were then seated and given the menus. This place has a large and diverse burger menu, with nine burger choices total. They serve coke products, and you have options for your sides. Besides the “Brew City” fries, you can instead order coleslaw, soup, BBQ baked beans, mac and cheese, or onion rings. Now even though the national website claims the fries are unlimited, that doesn’t seem to be the case here in Anchorage.

While here is our order, the Country Kitchen’s cheeseburger, and wow does it look good. The first thing you notice is the cheese since the burger is presented open faced. You will see two types of cheese melted on the beef patty. The burger comes with the usual PLOT but features red onions (*new fans → PLOT stands for Pickles Lettuce Onions & Tomatoes.) Once the burger was assembled I noticed no mayo. If you want mayo mention this to the server (I suggest mentioning it to the server while giving your food order). As far as condiments go mustard is brought with the ketchup. After a bit of mustard, the burger was ready.

The first bite into the burger was really good. The melted cheese really stands out with strong flavor along with the bun which according to the menu is an asiago bun. The bun really adds and helps to make this a really unique looking burger with great tasting too.

The fries which are unsalted go really well with the burger. In my opinion the fries also add to the overall presentation of the burger meal. This burger meal goes for $9.75 in downtown Anchorage, just within walking distance of the 5th Avenue Mall.

So far for Monday reviews this is one of our top burgers. At the end of the year December 2012 we will have our inaugural Alaska Top Burgers Awards. But we have 51 weeks to try 51 new cheeseburgers around the wonderful state of Alaska! So next week will play our chances and see if we get more than just a cheeseburger at our next burger $top.
