Weekly Burger Review. 01-08-2012.

Bacon Edition!

Welcome to a special Weekly Burger Review. Special not because it’s on Sunday rather than Friday. But Special because for 2012 once a month, the Weekly Burger Review will check out and compare Bacon Burgers. Let’s face the fact that just about every burger stop, dine in, and any place that serves burgers, has a bacon burger choice. So rather then consider it a specialty burger, once a month we do Weekly Burger Review Bacon Edition!

We had a baseball hat full of local eateries with bacon burgers, and the name we drew up, was Leroy’s Resturant! Leroy’s bacon Burger is called “The British Burger.” Interesting note, so far, we have found three places that named their bacon burger with English Names (England Burger, British Burger, and English Burger.)

Leroy’s located on C Street off Fireweed, is a local place serving breakfast all day, but we’re here to try the British Burger, our first bacon burger for the Alaska Burger Blog. The place serves Pepsi products with free refills. British Burger goes for $9.95. The Now here is our first look at the British Burger.

The British Burger comes with two strips of bacon, crinkle fries, mayo, and the usual PLOT. Added some mustard, and one bite, and it’s a good burger. The burger here reminded me of the old Princess, Phillips, and Birch Tree places that would serve breakfast all day. The bacon was really crispy but not overdone. Overall is a good bacon burger, and set a good bar for next month bacon burger.

If you know a place that serves an awesome bacon burger, let us know. Friday we’ll be back to regular Weekly Burger Review with a new place and new burger. Till Next Time, eat well.
